Tacit Continuity and Personal Knowledge (Michael Polanyi)

Overview

Michael Polanyi argues that judgment depends on tacit knowing: forms of understanding that cannot be fully articulated. In Personal Knowledge, he shows that judgment is personal, continuous, and time-formed.

We know more than we can tell.

The Tacit Dimension

Judgment relies on integrations that guide perception and action without being explicit.

These integrations form through time and cannot be transferred instantaneously.

Subsidiary and Focal Awareness

Judgment operates through subsidiary awareness supporting focal action.

This background is reshaped continuously through experience.

Personal Participation

Judgment involves commitment and responsibility. It cannot be detached from the knower without loss.

Personal does not mean subjective; it means time-formed.

Skill and Apprenticeship

Expert judgment arises through prolonged practice and correction.

Rules cannot substitute for this continuity.

Against Formalization

Attempts to formalize tacit knowledge often destroy it by shifting attention away from integration.

Judgment resists extraction.

Structural Implication

Judgment cannot be preserved by explicit knowledge alone. It depends on tacit continuity formed over time.

Preserve statements without participation, and judgment vanishes.
